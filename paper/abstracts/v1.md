# Abstract — v1

> Scientific AI models in pharmaceutics — including FormulationAI,
> PreformulationAI, PBPK-AI, and FormulationDT — are heterogeneous,
> siloed, and difficult to compose into multi-step research workflows.
> We present **FormulationOS**, an operating layer that turns natural
> language into executable, reproducible, and traceable scientific
> workflows. FormulationOS contributes five components: (i) the
> **Scientific Workflow Abstraction**, a first-class abstraction for
> scientific AI systems that unifies execution, persistence, replay,
> refinement, provenance, and artifacts under a single workflow
> object — internally represented as a directed acyclic execution
> graph in the current implementation; (ii) the **Scientific
> Workspace**, a persistent state model for cross-session research
> state; (iii) the **Execution Runtime**, which produces reproducible
> **scientific evidence chains** for every workflow execution; (iv)
> the **Scientific Registry**, an embedding-based tool catalog with
> embedding-based retrieval and a declarative tool specification
> (Scientific Tool Specification, STS) that augments standard tool
> descriptions with scientific semantics, dependencies, planning hints,
> and provenance hooks; and (v) an open-source prototype implementation and
> validation on five representative tools spanning computational
> pharmaceutics. FormulationOS does not promise deterministic models;
> it promises deterministic evidence — every scientific conclusion
> produced by the system is traceable to upstream inputs, tool
> versions, and compute environments.
